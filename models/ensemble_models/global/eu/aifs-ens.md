# AIFS ENS (Artificial Intelligence Forecasting System – Ensemble)

## What this model is
AIFS ENS is ECMWF's operational machine-learning-based global ensemble weather forecast system.

Like the deterministic [AIFS Single](./aifs-single.md), AIFS ENS is a trained neural network rather than a physics-based solver. Unlike AIFS Single, however, AIFS ENS is specifically trained to produce probabilistic forecasts using a CRPS-derived loss function (replaced by a multi-scale loss in v2) and generates ensemble members by injecting random Gaussian noise into the transformer processor during inference.

AIFS ENS runs operationally alongside the physics-based [IFS ENS](./ifs-ens.md) and is designed to complement rather than replace it. It became operational on 1 July 2025, making it ECMWF's second operational machine-learning forecast system after AIFS Single (operational 25 February 2025).

- **Organization:** European Centre for Medium-Range Weather Forecasts

## What area it covers
- **Coverage:** Global

## Basic details
- **Model type:** Global ensemble (AI-based)
- **Architecture:** Encoder–processor–decoder with attention-based graph neural networks (encoder/decoder) and a sliding-window transformer (processor) — same architecture as AIFS Single
- **Dynamical formulation:** Not applicable — AIFS ENS does not solve dynamical equations. It is a trained neural network that predicts atmospheric evolution directly from learned patterns in historical weather data.
- **Convection-allowing:** No (AIFS does not represent or parameterize convection explicitly; convective behaviour is implicit in what the network has learned at ~31 km resolution)
- **Training framework:** Anemoi (open-source AI-NWP framework co-developed with ECMWF Member States)
- **Training data:** ERA5 reanalysis (1979–2022) plus ECMWF operational analyses (2018 onwards for fine-tuning)
- **Model parameters:** ~229 million
- **Ensemble size:** 51 (1 control + 50 perturbed)
- **Native grid:** N320 reduced Gaussian grid (~31 km, ~0.25°)
- **Open Data resolution:** 0.25° regular latitude–longitude
- **Pressure levels:** 13 in v1 (50, 100, 150, 200, 250, 300, 400, 500, 600, 700, 850, 925, 1000 hPa); 14 in v2 (adds 10 hPa)
- **Forecast timestep:** 6 hours
- **Forecast length:** Up to 15 days
- **Update frequency:** 4× daily (00, 06, 12, 18 UTC)

## Initial conditions
AIFS ENS members are initialized member-by-member from **IFS ENS initial conditions**, not from a single shared analysis:

- **Control member:** Initialized from the **IFS control analysis** (the same unperturbed analysis used to initialize AIFS Single and the deterministic IFS), regridded from O1280 (~0.1°) to N320 (~0.25°).
- **Perturbed members:** Each AIFS ENS perturbed member is initialized from the corresponding **IFS ENS perturbed initial conditions** — i.e., the recentered EDA analyses with singular vector perturbations applied (the same initial perturbation strategy used by IFS ENS, see the [IFS ENS entry](./ifs-ens.md) for details on EDA + SV).

This means AIFS ENS inherits IFS's full initial-condition perturbation scheme and benefits from ECMWF's physics-based 4D-Var data assimilation system. AIFS ENS itself does not perform data assimilation.

---

## Perturbations and design
- **Initial condition perturbations:** Inherited from IFS ENS (recentered EDA + singular vectors). See above.
- **Model uncertainty:** Generated by injecting **independent Gaussian noise** into the transformer processor during inference. Each member receives a different random seed.
- **Notable design properties:**
  - Members are computed independently — the ensemble is fully parallelizable across GPUs.
  - The same trained neural network is used for all members; spread comes from differences in initial conditions and the injected noise, not from physics perturbations.
  - The "control" member is not a true control in the IFS-ENS sense — although it is initialized from the unperturbed IFS control analysis, it still has model noise injected during inference, so it produces a slightly different forecast each time it is run. Users comparing AIFS ENS control to AIFS Single should be aware that the two are not equivalent.
- **Loss function:** v1 used an "almost fair" CRPS (afCRPS) loss; v2 replaces this with a **multi-scale loss** (Lang, Leutbecher, and Maciel, 2025; arXiv:2506.10868) and applies the same physical-consistency variable bounding used by AIFS Single. The probabilistic training approach is designed to produce ensembles with realistic spread and atmospheric variability, avoiding the over-smoothing problem common in deterministically-trained (MSE) AI forecast models.

## What it provides
Probabilistic global forecasts including:
- Individual ensemble member forecasts (50 perturbed + 1 control)
- Ensemble mean and ensemble spread
- Surface and upper-air fields: temperature, wind components (10 m, 100 m, upper-air), geopotential height, specific humidity, vertical velocity, mean sea level pressure, total column water
- Total precipitation (and convective precipitation in v2), snowfall
- Cloud cover (total, high, mid, low)
- Surface short-wave and long-wave radiation downwards
- Soil moisture and soil temperature (two layers)

AIFS ENS v2 (operational 12 May 2026) additionally provides:
- A new pressure level at **10 hPa** for geopotential, temperature, U, V, and vertical velocity (improving stratospheric representation, including sudden stratospheric warmings)
- A new **fraction of snow cover** parameter (FSCOV)
- **Convective precipitation (CP)** and **volumetric soil moisture (VSW)** as new outputs, harmonizing with AIFS Single
- A new **wave ensemble stream** providing significant wave height, mean wave period, mean wave direction, drag coefficient, and significant wave height partitioned across six period bands (10–12, 12–14, 14–17, 17–21, 21–25, and 25–30 seconds). This is the first time AIFS produces probabilistic ocean wave forecasts.

## Relationship to other models
AIFS ENS is the **ensemble AI companion** to ECMWF's physics-based [IFS ENS](./ifs-ens.md). It runs operationally alongside IFS ENS and is considered complementary rather than a replacement.

It is the probabilistically-trained counterpart to **[AIFS Single](./aifs-single.md)** (the deterministic AI model). Both share the same underlying encoder–processor–decoder architecture but differ in training methodology, loss function, and the addition of stochastic noise injection in AIFS ENS.

AIFS ENS depends on **IFS** for initial conditions — it inherits IFS ENS's perturbed initial conditions on a member-by-member basis and relies on ECMWF's physics-based 4D-Var data assimilation system to generate the analyses, then propagates forecasts forward using the neural network.

ECMWF stops running its experimental machine learning model suite (Aurora, FourCastNet, GraphCast, Pangu-Weather) as of the joint AIFS/IFS upgrade on 12 May 2026.

## Version history

### AIFS ENS v1 — operational 1 July 2025
First operational version of the AIFS ensemble. Replaced earlier research configurations including AIFS–Diffusion (12-hour timestep) and early AIFS–CRPS (6-hour timestep). v1 used the afCRPS ("almost fair" CRPS) loss function.

Training: pre-training on ERA5 (1979–2022, 300,000 steps), then fine-tuning on ERA5 + IFS operational analyses (2018–2023). Initial conditions derived from IFS ENS perturbations on a member-by-member basis; ensemble spread generated through injected Gaussian noise during inference.

### AIFS ENS v2 — scheduled 12 May 2026
Major upgrade deployed jointly with [IFS Cycle 50r1](./ifs.md#upcoming-changes) and [AIFS Single v2](./aifs-single.md). Architectural and scientific changes:

- **New 10 hPa pressure level** for geopotential, temperature, horizontal winds, and vertical velocity (consistent with AIFS Single v2).
- **New wave ensemble stream** (`stream=waef`) — the first probabilistic data-driven wave forecasts ECMWF has issued.
- **New parameters:** fraction of snow cover (FSCOV), convective precipitation (CP), volumetric soil moisture (VSW). The latter two harmonize AIFS ENS output with AIFS Single.
- **Multi-scale loss function** replaces the afCRPS loss used in v1, improving physical consistency at multiple spatial scales (Lang, Leutbecher, and Maciel, 2025; arXiv:2506.10868).
- **Variable bounding** matching AIFS Single is now applied, improving physical consistency.
- **Revised graph features** — the decoder now uses more edges with new edge features.
- **Improved training regime** — fine-tuning extended by one year (2018–2024 vs 2018–2023) including IFS Cycle 50r1 esuite analyses, over 7,900 fine-tuning training steps. Pre-training is unchanged from v1 (ERA5 1979–2022 over 300,000 steps).
- **Technical fix:** corrects a `productDefinitionTemplateNumber` GRIB2 encoding issue affecting some step 0 parameters in v1.
- **Dissemination priority changes** — AIFS ENS atmospheric output priority drops from 90 to 40 on ECPDS; the new wave stream uses priority 50 (matching IFS ENS-WAM).

The release candidate phase started 14 April 2026; test data is available via MARS (expver=105) and the open data testdata endpoint. v2 is being upgraded jointly with IFS Cycle 50r1 because v1 shows degraded performance when initialized from 50r1 esuite analyses — v2 has been fine-tuned specifically on 50r1 data.

## Notes
- AIFS ENS generates forecasts more than 10× faster than the physics-based IFS ENS, with approximately 1,000× lower energy consumption per forecast.
- A single AIFS ensemble member running on one NVIDIA A100 GPU produces a 15-day forecast in roughly 2.5 minutes, compared to ~1 hour on 96 AMD Epyc Rome CPUs for an equivalent physics-based ensemble member.
- Known limitations include underprediction of extreme precipitation maxima (more pronounced than in IFS ENS), reduced effective resolution in cloud fields, and continued limited skill in the tropical stratosphere. AIFS ENS has shown skill improvements of up to 25% over IFS ENS for upper-air variables, with positive impacts on surface variables including 2 m temperature and total precipitation.
- At ~31 km resolution, AIFS ENS is coarser than the ~9 km IFS ENS, which can affect representation of extreme precipitation events and high-resolution features.
- Individual ensemble members do not exhibit the smoothing seen in AIFS Single's deterministic forecasts — the noise injection during inference produces members with similar levels of forecast activity to IFS ENS, even though each member uses the same underlying trained network.
- Due to the non-determinism of GPU computation, users running AIFS ENS themselves cannot exactly reproduce ECMWF's official forecasts.
- Open Data distribution is at AIFS ENS's native resolution. Higher-resolution AIFS ENS products are not produced; the ~31 km resolution is the operational resolution.
